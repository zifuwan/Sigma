PISA: Task-Oriented Part Segmentation with Language Reasoning

Zifu Wan, Yaqi Xie, Ce Zhang, Zhiqiu Lin, Zihan Wang, Simon Stepputtis, Deva Ramanan, Katia Sycara
Robotics Institute, Carnegie Mellon University
In Submission
Paper Supplementary Data Code

Highlights

  • We propose the novel Task Reasoning Part Segmentation (TRPS) task and a dataset consisting of hand-labeled real-world images paired with curated task descriptions.
  • We benchmark state-of-the-art VLMs on our dataset and reveal their limitations. To address these, we introduce PISA, a robust new baseline for our benchmark.
  • By training with our 1,800 samples, PISA gains a performance boost of over twofold. The model is further evaluated on a real-world grasping case study and outperforms current SOTA methods.

Abstract

Large multimodal foundation models, particularly in the domain of language and vision, have greatly accelerated many tasks, including robotics, autonomous driving, information retrieval, and grounding. However, many of these models perceive objects as indivisible, neglecting the components comprising a whole. Yet, knowing about the various components and their associated affordances provides valuable insights into the utility of an object, which can be fundamental to performing various tasks. In this work, we propose a novel real-world benchmark, PISA, consisting of hand-labeled object-part images and two associated tasks: a) identifying an object part given a task-oriented instruction and b) identifying an object part given a part query. Through our experiments, we show that task-oriented part segmentation remains a challenging task even for recent state-of-the-art Vision-Language Models (VLM). In addition to our benchmark, we also introduce a new baseline method and demonstrate its strong generalization ability to robot manipulation tasks. With our dataset, associated benchmark, and baseline, we hope to facilitate studies on task-oriented part segmentation and advance research in many domains, including robotics, virtual reality, information retrieval, and other related disciplines.

InstructPart Dataset and Tasks

InstructPart dataset: InstructPart dataset comprises of 2,400 images across 48 object classes and 44 part classes, with hand-labeled segmentation masks, as well as 14,400 hand-labeled task instructions, part queries, and affordances. We split the dataset into 1,800 images for training and 600 images for evaluation.

Task Reasoning Part Segmentation (TRPS): To explore the reasoning and part grounding ability of current models, we propose the TRPS task, which is shown in the first row of Figure 2. The models should only take a task-image pair as the input and find the part segmentation masks being referred to.

Oracle Referring Part Segmentation (ORPS): In the ORPS setting, we consider using the part name to directly refer to the part, which ensures the model has a correct text input. The task is shown in the second row of Figure 2.
